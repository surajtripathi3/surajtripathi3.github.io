<a>
<papertitle>AMUSED: A Multi-Stream Vector Representation Method for Use In Natural Dialogue</papertitle>
</a>
<br>
<strong>Gaurav Kumar</strong>, Rishabh Joshi, Jaspreet Singh, Promod Yenigalla
<br>
<p></p>
<p>Goal is to build a coherent and non-monotonous conversational agent with proper
discourse and coverage. We propose an end to end multi-stream deep learning
architecture which learns unified embeddings
for query-response pairs by leveraging contextual information from memory networks and
syntactic information by incorporating Graph
Convolution Networks (GCN) over their dependency parse. A stream of this network
also utilizes transfer learning by pre-training
a bidirectional transformer to extract semantic representation for each input sentence and
incorporates external knowledge through the
neighborhood of the entities from a Knowledge Base (KB). We benchmark these embeddings on next sentence prediction task and
significantly improve upon the existing techniques.
</p>

<papertitle>Understanding physical properties of objects by visual cues using neural networks </papertitle>
</a>
<br>
Research Work at <a href="https://www.cin.uni-tuebingen.de/">Centre for Integrative Neuroscience</a>,
<a href="https://uni-tuebingen.de/">University of Tubingen</a>
<br>
<p></p>
<p>
<li>Worked on varying cloth videos to understand their material properties based on certain external parameters.</li>

<li>Experimented with CNN+LSTM networks, 2 stream, multi loss networks along with optical flow for feature extraction</li>
<li>Proposed triplet loss function for videos similar to FaceNet paper to get their low dimension representation.</li>
<li>Results showed strong correlation between human perception of physical properties and neural networks in case of motion with the perceptual graph being logarithmic. Work published in Journal of Vision 2019.</li>
</p>

<a >
<papertitle>Converting Handwritten Mathematical equations to LaTeX</papertitle>
</a>
<br>
Course project, Image Modeling Techniques: Supervised by <a href="http://tanayag.com/Home.html">Dr. Tanaya Guha</a>
<br>
[<a href="data/latex_ppt.pdf">Slides</a>]/[<a href="data/latex_report.pdf">Report</a>]
<br>
<p>
<li>Developed a deep learning based pipeline to convert handwritten mathematical equations to LaTeX from scratch.</li>

<li>Incorporated edge detectors, Hough Transform along with other segmentation methods for pre-processing the image and built a novel tree structure based algorithm to identify multilevel superscripts and subscripts.</li>
<li>Trained multiple classifiers including SVM's, CNN, Random Forests for symbol recognition and reached accuracies close to 98% even on complex trigonometric symbols. </li>
<li>Awarded best project out of 20 others.</li>
</p>

<papertitle>Captcha Breaking</papertitle>
</a>
<br>
Course project, Machine Learning Techniques, supervised by <a href="https://www.cse.iitk.ac.in/users/purushot/">Dr. Purushottam Kar</a>
<br>
[<a href="data/mlt_ppt.pdf">Slides</a>]

<p>
<li>Objective was to build an efficient algorithm to break online squirrel mail client captchas using neural networks</li>
<li>Performed feature engineering methods such as clustering, dominating color based segmentation to remove heavy noise and orthogonal cluttering through captcha text before feeding it to a character recognizer.</li>

<li>Implemented variants of CNN models from scratch for character recognition and reached 98% accuracy. Entire Captcha breaking reached 85% accuracy for very noisy captchas and 98% for less noisy captchas.</li>
